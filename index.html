THE CORE DISCOVERY

Understanding the CFAD Principle

  • Dynamic Inertial Equilibrium: A mechanical state in which the internal tension force cannot relax in the classical manner, requiring a dynamic response.
  • Kinematic Feedback & Lock: The specific arrangement of gears, chains and supports creates a closed-loop constraint that permanently traps the internal tension force.
  • Geometry and Built-in Tension as the Driver: The resulting acceleration depends on the system's geometry (radii ratio) and built-in tension magnitude, independent of external energy input.
  • Negative Effective Moment of Inertia: A key condition for self-excitation, where one element (the carrier) behaves as a dynamic support within the system's equilibrium.
  • Cyclic Operation for Practicality: The principle is designed for a feasible cycle of acceleration and regenerative braking, resulting in usable electrical or mechanical energy.
