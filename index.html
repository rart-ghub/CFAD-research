A New Paradigm in Newtonian Mechanics

The Carrier Feedback Acceleration Drive (CFAD) demonstrates that a specific kinematic coupling can lock a mechanical system in a state of inherent dynamic imbalance. The only consistent physical response is continuous acceleration, governed by geometry, not by an external energy input.

This is not a violation of physics, but a deepening of it—a new paradigm for motion and energy within closed mechanical systems.

THE CORE DISCOVERY

Understanding the CFAD Principle

⚖️

Inherent Dynamic Imbalance

A mechanical state where internal forces cannot relax, demanding a dynamic response.

🔗

Kinematic Feedback & Lock

A specific arrangement of gears, chains, and a carrier creates a closed-loop constraint that traps elastic tension.

📐

Geometry as the Driver

The resulting continuous acceleration is dictated by the system's physical dimensions (radii, distances), not by the magnitude of the input tension.

🔋

Negative Effective Inertia

A key condition for self-excitation, where one element (the carrier) behaves as a dynamic energy source within the system's balance.

🔄

Cyclic Operation for Practicality

The principle is designed for a feasible cycle of acceleration and regenerative braking, transforming inherent imbalance into usable kinetic energy.
